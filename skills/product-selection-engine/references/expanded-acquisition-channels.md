# Expanded Acquisition Channels

Use this reference when the user wants broader, deeper, more professional product research rather than a narrow trend-only scan.

Use the shared monitoring standard from `../../self-cognition-orchestrator/references/monitoring-and-retrofit-framework.md`.

## Objective

Increase the breadth, strength, and detail of product-selection evidence by pulling from multiple source families and then synthesizing them cleanly.

## Source families

### 1. Trend sources
- Google Trends
- Pinterest Trends
- public trend roundups
- recurring search-theme pages

### 2. Marketplace sources
- search results pages
- category pages
- bestseller-style pages
- product pages
- related-product surfaces
- offer/variant/review patterns

### 3. Search and discovery sources
- autocomplete
- related searches
- people-also-ask style public surfaces
- public keyword roundups
- category keyword clusters
- Pinterest boards/pins as visual-demand and idea surfaces
- Etsy search/category pages as niche-demand and customization surfaces

### 4. Community/problem sources
- Reddit
- public forums
- Q&A pages
- enthusiast communities
- parenting/home/organization/auto communities depending on niche
- places where people describe a need without knowing the product name
- places where people say existing tools do not solve the problem well

### 5. Review and comparison sources
- public product comparisons
- gift guides
- buying guides
- review summaries
- “best X for Y” pages

### 6. Feasibility sources
- supplier catalogs
- manufacturer capability pages
- product-spec comparison pages
- public wholesale/distribution patterns

## What each source family is good for

- Trend sources -> macro interest and momentum
- Marketplace sources -> real commercial behavior clues
- Search/discovery sources -> buyer phrasing and intent
- Community/problem sources -> pain points and unmet needs
- Review/comparison sources -> differentiation and complaint structure
- Feasibility sources -> producibility and sourcing reality

## Professional analysis outputs to extract

For each candidate, try to produce:
- signal summary by source family
- stable-demand hypothesis
- competition survivability hypothesis
- top buyer problem statement
- abstracted selling point
- non-infringing comparable path
- manufacturability judgment
- final confidence

## Synthesis rule

Do not just stack sources. Convert them into a clean narrative:

1. what the market seems to want
2. what the product does for the buyer
3. how crowded the niche is
4. why the candidate might still survive
5. what still needs verification


## Fuzzy-demand interpretation layer

Treat these as valuable signals:
- explicit “I need a product/tool that can…” requests
- “Where can I find…” questions
- complaints that current products are close but not good enough
- repeated DIY workarounds, hacks, or custom modifications

These signals can indicate a product opportunity even when the market is not expressing the demand with one clean product keyword yet.
