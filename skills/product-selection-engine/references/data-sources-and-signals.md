# Data Sources and Signals

Use the shared monitoring standard from `../../self-cognition-orchestrator/references/monitoring-and-retrofit-framework.md` when deciding whether signals are strong enough, conflicting, stale, or in need of a backstop review.

## Public methodology sources

Look for:
- public product-research frameworks
- marketplace seller advice
- public selection checklists
- public niche-evaluation articles/videos/posts

Goal:
- extract reusable criteria, not copy claims blindly

## Trend sources

Prioritize:
- Google Trends
- Pinterest Trends
- Reddit
- public platform trend pages
- platform search suggestions / autocomplete when available

Monitor for:
- recency
- seasonality distortion
- vanity/noise signals

## Marketplace/product-page signals

Collect public clues such as:
- review count
- visible recent-sales hints
- bestseller/ranking hints
- price range
- listing count / saturation clues
- variant count
- listing similarity
- new-product volume clues

Monitor for:
- shallow popularity without demand quality
- incomplete platform visibility
- copied/saturated listing patterns

## Competition clues

Watch for:
- too many lookalike listings
- compressed price bands
- weak differentiation
- saturated keyword space

## Business viability clues

Check:
- shipping weight/size burden
- fragility
- return complexity
- support complexity
- certification/compliance needs


## Exclusion and abstraction checks

When reviewing signals, also check:

- is this signal pointing to a food/ingestible product that should be excluded by default?
- is this signal tied to a protected brand, franchise, character, or trade dress?
- can the attractive feature be abstracted into a non-infringing selling point?
- does the market show evidence that similar non-infringing products already exist?
- does current manufacturing/sourcing appear capable of producing a comparable concept?


## Amazon / Walmart branch signal preference

When the target platform is Amazon or Walmart, emphasize signals of:

- stable ongoing demand
- moderate but durable sales activity
- niches with room for smaller sellers
- low brand dominance
- low regulatory / formulation / claims burden

De-emphasize:

- pure social virality
- strong brand-led categories
- regulated health/body-contact categories
- products that look like they require heavy review moats or brand trust to enter


## Main-trunk optimization note

For Amazon and Walmart style selection, also read `main-trunk-acquisition-optimization.md` so the system filters noisy categories earlier instead of wasting effort before scoring.


## Additional source channels to actively use

Do not limit acquisition to trend pages and a few marketplace pages. Also consider:

- search autocomplete and related searches
- Reddit threads and niche forums for repeated buyer language
- public comparison pages and roundup articles
- marketplace category pages, subcategory pages, and related-product surfaces
- public supplier/manufacturer catalog patterns for feasibility clues
- recurring complaint/review themes that reveal product gaps

## Depth expectation

For stronger runs, aim to answer not only:
- Is demand present?

But also:
- What exact problem is being solved?
- How do buyers describe it?
- Why do adjacent products win or lose?
- What structural feature seems to matter most?
- Is the opportunity supported by multiple independent signal families?


## Additional demand-discovery surfaces

Also actively consider:
- Pinterest for repeated visual themes, aesthetics, and product-use inspiration
- Etsy for niche demand, handmade-style ideas, customization patterns, and smaller-problem products
- forums/communities where people ask for a tool or product that does not quite exist yet

Do not only search for named products. Search for unmet or partially met demand language too.
